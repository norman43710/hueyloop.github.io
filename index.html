Where It Began

Why Huey Loop?

Some places become more than a location. They become part of who we are.

A Real Place

An old Army hangar on Huey Loop at Simmons Army Airfield became the starting point of a much larger story.

Huey Loop is more than a name. It is a real place.

In 2006, I walked into an old Army hangar on Huey Loop at Simmons Army Airfield and began my journey in military aviation. At the time, I thought I was simply beginning a career. Looking back, I believe God was beginning a much greater story.

Over the years, I served alongside remarkable people. Together we experienced hardship, sacrifice, laughter, friendship, success, failure, and moments that shaped our lives in ways we could not fully appreciate at the time.

Years later, after deployments, leadership opportunities, friendships, blessings, and countless lessons, I found myself standing once again where it all began.

Back on Huey Loop.

Standing there, I realized something that has never left me. The buildings were never what made that place special.

The people did.

The conversations. The friendships. The mentors. The families. The sacrifices. The faith. The memories. The stories.

That realization became the foundation of Huey Loop. This business exists because I believe the greatest legacy we leave behind is not found in possessions or titles, but in the lives we touch and the stories we preserve.

Places may begin our stories, but people give them meaning.

Why Stories Matter

We hope our work inspires you to preserve the stories of the people you love.

Huey Loop documents the stories of our own friends, family members, mentors, and loved ones. We do not collect recordings or ask visitors to upload their personal stories to this website.

Instead, we hope the conversations shared here encourage others to sit down with the people they love, ask meaningful questions, and preserve those memories in whatever way feels right for their own family.

It does not require professional equipment. Sometimes all it takes is a phone, a quiet room, and the willingness to listen.

Explore Our Stories
  • Call someone whose story matters to you
  • Ask about childhood and family traditions
  • Talk about faith, work, hardship, and joy
  • Write down names connected to old photographs
  • Save the stories behind family keepsakes
  • Do not wait for the perfect time to begin

Our Promise

Huey Loop is a Christian, military family-owned business built on faith, integrity, and a commitment to preserving stories that matter.
